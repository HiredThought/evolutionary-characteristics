Introduction: What is a Component?

A component may be an Activity, a Practice, Data, or Knowledge.

For example, a Cup of Tea could be a component. It might need additional underlying components such as Tea and Hot Water.

Introduction: What is Evolution?

All components evolve from left to right under the influence of supply and demand competition.

Genesis

Unique, rare, uncertain, constantly changing, newly-discovered.

The focus is on exploring.

Custom

Very uncommon. Tailor-made for a specific environment. Frequently changes. Requires artisanal skill. No two are the same.

The focus is on learning and developing the craft

Product (and rental)

Increasingly common, more defined, better understood. Repeatable manufacturing process. Change is slower. Initial differentiation but increasing stability and sameness. Often many of the same product. Improvement.

The focus is on refining and improving.

Commodity (and utility)

Scale and volume operations of production. Highly standardised. Defined. Fixed. Undifferentiated. Fit for a specific known purpose. Repetition, repetition, repetition... With time, it becomes commonplace and more invisible.

The focus is on ruthlessly removing deviation, industrialising, and increasing operational efficiently.
